I finished Elden Ring a little while back, and I've had time to sit with it.
Short version: it's one of the best open-world games I've played. Long
version below.

## Level design carries the whole game

The verticality is what gets me most. You'll see a tower from across the
map, climb toward it for twenty minutes, and the path there reveals three
other things you didn't expect — a hidden cave, a boss arena, an NPC quest
thread. It's the kind of design that rewards curiosity constantly, not just
at scripted moments.

## The difficulty spikes are real

Some bosses are brutal in a way that occasionally tips from "tense and
fair" into "frustrating." A couple of late-game fights felt like they
wanted you to summon help or grind levels rather than out-skill them. It
didn't ruin the experience, but it's worth knowing going in.

## Would I recommend it?

Yes, easily — especially if you liked the sense of discovery in earlier
Soulsborne games and want that scaled up to an open world. Budget more
time than you think you'll need.
